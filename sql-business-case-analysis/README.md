# Sql Business Case Analysis
Or main goal in this project is to answer business question using SQL on sales data.
This inspections shows how SQL can be utilized to evaluate revenue performance, sales trend and csutomer contribution to support data-driven decision making.

# Business Questions
1. What is the overall revenue generated?
2. What is the average order value?
3. Which categories contributes the most revenue?
4. Which customers generates the highest revenue?
5. How does revenue trend over time?
6. Which categories have high order volume but relatively low revenue?

# Data
- Values such as order value(selling price), date, customer and category were included.
- Dataset was scoped to focus on revenue and customer analysis.

# Tools Used
- SQL is the core tool used for aggregation and analysis.

# Key Analysis
- Calculated total revenue and average order value.
- Inspected the revenue share by category and customer.
- Evaluated sales trend over time.
- Identified high frequency but low revenue categories.

# Key Takeaways
- Revenue is dominated by one categpry, while other catgeroies with similar performance indicates a competitive distribution share.
- Few customers contribute more to the total revenue indicating a concentration risk.
- Revenue overall stable, faces periodic fluctuations indicating a predictable sales behaviour. 
- Some categories have high order total but lower revenue contribution, indicating a potential pricing inefficiencies.
